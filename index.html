Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 6 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley or dill) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Salmon:

    In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, Dijon mustard, lemon zest, salt, and pepper to create the marinade. Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish and pour half of the marinade over them. Make sure the fillets are well-coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator. Reserve the other half of the marinade for basting.

  2. Preheat the Grill:

    Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Make sure the grates are clean and lightly oiled to prevent sticking.

  3. Prepare the Salmon for Grilling:

    Remove the marinated salmon from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature for about 10-15 minutes. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels to remove excess marinade. This helps in achieving a good sear on the grill.

  4. Grill the Salmon:

    Place the salmon fillets on the preheated grill, skin side down. Close the lid and grill for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets. Baste the salmon with the reserved marinade while grilling.

  5. Check for Doneness:

    The salmon is done when it easily flakes with a fork. Be careful not to overcook, as salmon can dry out quickly.

  6. Serve:

    Carefully remove the grilled salmon from the grill and transfer it to a serving platter. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs if desired and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Prerequisites:

  • Grill: Make sure your grill is clean and properly preheated.
  • Marinade: Allow enough time for the salmon to marinate to enhance flavor.
  • Tongs or a spatula for flipping the salmon on the grill.

Importance of Grilled Salmon:

  • Nutrient-Rich: Salmon is a great source of high-quality protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and various vitamins and minerals.
  • Heart Health: The omega-3 fatty acids in salmon are known to promote heart health and reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Versatility: Grilled salmon can be paired with a variety of sides, making it a versatile dish that can be customized to suit different preferences.
  • Quick and Healthy: Grilling is a quick and healthy cooking method that preserves the nutritional value of the salmon while adding a delicious smoky flavor.
  • Flavorful: The marinade and grilling process enhance the natural flavor of the salmon, making it a tasty and enjoyable dish.
